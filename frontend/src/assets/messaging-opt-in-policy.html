Messaging & Opt-In Policy

Last updated: November 25, 2025

This Messaging & Opt-In Policy explains how TSQS INC (“we”, “us”, “our”) expects its clients (“Clients”) to collect, store, and use consent when sending campaigns through Camply. Our platform supports messaging via SMS (Twilio), WhatsApp and Instagram Direct Messages (Meta), and email (via integrated email providers).

By uploading contacts or sending campaigns using our platform, you agree to follow this policy and all applicable laws and carrier guidelines (including but not limited to TCPA, CTIA, CAN-SPAM, GDPR, CCPA, and Meta & Twilio messaging policies).

1. Permission-Based Messaging Only

Our platform is intended for permission-based communications. You may only message individuals who have given explicit, informed consent to receive messages from your organization through the channels you use (SMS, WhatsApp, Instagram Direct Messages, and/or email).

2. Acceptable Forms of Opt-In

Consent must be obtained directly by you (the Client) and must clearly disclose:

  • Who is sending the messages (your brand or organization name), and
  • What type of messages they will receive (e.g., offers, alerts, updates), and
  • Which channels you will use (SMS, WhatsApp, Instagram DM, email), and
  • How often you may contact them, and how they can opt out.

Examples of valid opt-in methods include:

  • Online signup forms with a clear consent statement and checkbox.
  • Checkout or registration forms where subscribers explicitly agree to receive marketing messages.
  • In-person or paper forms that clearly state the above and are stored as records.
  • Double opt-in flows (e.g., “click to confirm” email or “reply YES to confirm” SMS).

Opt-in must never be hidden in terms & conditions or pre-checked boxes. The subscriber must take a clear, affirmative action to consent.

3. Channel-Specific Consent

You must have consent appropriate for each messaging channel. A subscriber may choose to receive messages on some channels and not others.

  • SMS (Text Messaging) – You must obtain express consent to send SMS messages to the subscriber’s phone number. CTIA and carrier rules apply.
  • WhatsApp – You must obtain explicit opt-in to receive messages on WhatsApp for your brand, following Meta’s commerce and messaging policies.
  • Instagram Direct Messages – You may only send messages to users who have initiated conversation with you or who have opted in through a clearly disclosed form.
  • Email – You must have permission to email the subscriber and comply with CAN-SPAM and other applicable regulations.

Our platform stores channel-level consent flags (SMS, WhatsApp, Instagram DM, email) and may block sending where consent has not been recorded.

4. Prohibited Practices

You may not use our platform to send messages to:

  • Purchased, rented, or scraped contact lists.
  • Individuals who have not explicitly agreed to receive your messages.
  • Individuals who have previously unsubscribed or opted out.
  • Contacts collected for one purpose but used for unrelated marketing without additional consent.

We reserve the right to suspend or terminate messaging access if we detect high complaint rates, spam-like behavior, or other violations of this policy.

5. Proof of Consent & Record-Keeping

When you upload contacts to Camply, you will be asked to confirm that all contacts in the CSV have explicitly opted in for the channels you intend to use. You may upload proof of consent (e.g., screenshots, form URLs, process descriptions), which we store securely for compliance purposes.

You are responsible for maintaining your own records of consent (e.g., form submissions, logs, timestamps). If carriers, providers (such as Twilio or Meta), or regulators request evidence of opt-in, it is your responsibility to provide it.

6. Opt-Out and Unsubscribe

You must provide clear and simple ways for recipients to stop receiving messages and honor those requests promptly.

  • SMS: support standard keywords such as “STOP” to unsubscribe.
  • WhatsApp / Instagram: respect user messages requesting to stop, blocks, or other clear opt-out actions.
  • Email: include a visible unsubscribe link in each marketing email.

Once a subscriber has opted out on any channel, you must not send further messages on that channel through our platform.

7. Data Privacy & Security

We process contact data on your behalf as a service provider / processor. We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect contact data stored in our systems. For more details, please review our Privacy Policy.

8. Client Responsibilities

As our Client, you agree that you will:

  • Collect, store, and manage consent in compliance with all applicable laws.
  • Provide accurate information when confirming opt-in and uploading proof.
  • Only send messages that are consistent with what subscribers were told at the time of opt-in.
  • Promptly update opt-out preferences in your systems.
  • Cooperate with any reasonable compliance review or audit.

9. Our Role

We provide tools to capture, store, and enforce consent settings, but we do not act as your legal advisor and we do not independently verify every proof of opt-in you upload. You remain fully responsible for your own compliance practices and for ensuring that your use of our platform is lawful.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Messaging & Opt-In Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of this page. Your continued use of the platform after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
